About UB Network Diagnostics

A network troubleshooting service for the University at Buffalo community, operated by UB Information Technology.

What this service shows

This site displays the Internet Protocol address visible to UB services. Depending on your connection and the information available from campus network systems, it may also show:

  • IPv4 and IPv6 connectivity.
  • Whether the address is recognized as on-campus, off-campus or connected through a campus VPN.
  • DNS resolver and DNS security information.
  • Campus network, VLAN, DHCP and IP address management details.
  • Additional device or connection information when supported by UB network management systems.

Get help

When contacting support, use the Report button on the home page to download a summary of the connection details shown by this service.

Privacy and appropriate use

The service processes network and browser information needed to produce diagnostic results. Operational and usage data may be recorded for service reliability, security and troubleshooting.

See the University at Buffalo Website Privacy Policy for information about data collected by official UB websites and associated web applications.

Open-source foundation

This deployment is adapted from the What Is My IP? project developed by UNC Information Technology Services and released under the MIT License.

UB-specific branding, campus network ranges and integrations are maintained by the University at Buffalo deployment team.
